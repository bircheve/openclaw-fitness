# Training Philosophy

A blend of classic bodybuilding principles inspired by Chris Bumstead and Sam Sulek, adapted for sustainable lean physique development.

## Core Tenets

### 1. Mind-Muscle Connection Over Ego

Weight is a tool, not a scorecard. The goal is to stimulate the target muscle, not move the most weight possible. Every rep should be felt in the intended muscle.

- Control the weight through the entire range of motion
- If you can't feel the target muscle, the weight is too heavy
- Quality reps beat quantity every time

### 2. Controlled Tempo

Slow eccentrics build muscle and prevent injury.

- 3-5 second lowering phase on most movements
- Full range of motion always (no partials unless intentional technique)
- No bouncing, swinging, or momentum to move the weight
- Brief pause at the stretch position to eliminate momentum

### 3. Volume and Intensity Balance

Find the sweet spot between enough volume to grow and enough intensity to stimulate.

- 8-12 working sets per muscle group per session
- 12-15 rep range for isolation movements
- 8-12 rep range for compound movements
- Last set to failure on most exercises
- Use techniques sparingly: drop sets, supersets, rest-pause

### 4. Progressive Overload via Double Progression

Strength gains drive muscle gains. See [double-progression.md](double-progression.md) for the full method.

- Start at the bottom of the rep range
- Build reps session over session
- Increase weight only when hitting top of range on all sets
- Small, consistent jumps beat large sporadic ones

### 5. Consistency Over Perfection

Show up every day. A mediocre workout beats a skipped workout.

- 7 days/week in the gym
- Every session ends with 30-45 min stairmaster
- Show up even when motivation is low
- Bad days still count as progress

### 6. Cardio as Non-Negotiable

The stairmaster is not optional. It's part of every session.

- Cardiovascular health supports recovery and longevity
- Calorie expenditure supports lean physique goals
- Mental discipline built through consistent effort
- 30-45 minutes at moderate intensity (130-150 BPM)

## Training Style Influences

### Chris Bumstead
- Classic bodybuilding aesthetics
- Controlled, deliberate movements
- Mind-muscle connection priority
- Full range of motion
- 3-5 second eccentrics

### Sam Sulek
- High volume training
- Intensity techniques (failure, drop sets)
- No scheduled rest days
- Training to absolute failure
- 8-12 reps for hypertrophy

## Session Structure

Every workout follows the same flow:

1. **Warmup** (10-15 min): [warmup-protocol.md](warmup-protocol.md)
2. **Lifting** (45-75 min): Execute the day's routine
3. **Cardio Finisher** (30-45 min): Stairmaster at moderate intensity

## Recovery Principles

Training hard requires recovering hard.

- Sleep: 7-8 hours minimum
- Nutrition: Hit protein targets daily
- Hydration: 1+ gallon water daily
- Deload: Every 4-6 weeks or when progress stalls
