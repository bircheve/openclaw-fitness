# Lat Pulldown

Foundational vertical pulling movement for lat width development.

## Why This Exercise

- Primary lat width builder
- Easier to learn than pull-ups
- Allows precise loading for progressive overload
- Great for mind-muscle connection with lats

## Grip Variations

| Grip | Emphasis |
|------|----------|
| Wide overhand | Lat width (standard) |
| Close neutral | Lat thickness, more bicep |
| Underhand (chin-up grip) | Lower lats, more bicep |
| Single arm | Unilateral work, full stretch |

## Mind-Muscle Connection Tips

The key to this exercise is feeling your lats, not your biceps.

1. Think about pulling your elbows into your back pockets
2. Initiate with shoulder blades (depress and retract)
3. Hands are just hooks - don't squeeze the bar
4. At the bottom, imagine crushing something in your armpits
5. Use a thumbless grip to reduce bicep involvement

## Common Programming

- 3-4 sets of 10-12 reps
- Use as a primary back exercise on pull days
- Superset with straight arm pulldowns for a pump

## Variations

- Wide grip pulldown (standard)
- Close grip pulldown
- Single arm pulldown
- Behind neck pulldown (not recommended - shoulder stress)
- Straight arm pulldown (isolation version)
