# Barbell Back Squat

The king of leg exercises. Foundational compound movement for lower body development.

## Why This Exercise

- Most effective quad and glute builder
- Builds overall leg mass and strength
- Strengthens core and spinal erectors
- Functional movement pattern
- Allows for heavy progressive overload

## Bar Position

| Position | Characteristics |
|----------|-----------------|
| High bar | On traps, more upright, quad dominant |
| Low bar | On rear delts, more forward lean, posterior chain |

Most bodybuilders prefer high bar for quad emphasis.

## Depth Requirements

At minimum, hit parallel (hip crease at or below top of knee).

Going deeper is fine if mobility allows, but don't sacrifice form for depth:
- Some butt wink is okay
- Excessive rounding is not
- If you can't hit depth, work on ankle/hip mobility

## Breathing and Bracing

1. Take a big breath at the top
2. Brace core hard (like someone's going to punch you)
3. Descend while holding breath
4. Drive up
5. Exhale at or near the top
6. Repeat

## Warmup Protocol

Essential before heavy squats:
1. Bodyweight squats x 15
2. Bar only x 15
3. 50% working weight x 10
4. 70% working weight x 8
5. 85% working weight x 5

## Common Programming

- 3-4 sets of 8-10 reps for hypertrophy
- Primary movement on quad-focused leg days
- Can superset with leg curls for time efficiency

## Variations

- Front Squat (more quad dominant)
- Hack Squat Machine (similar pattern, less stability demand)
- Leg Press (if squat isn't possible)
- Goblet Squat (learning tool, lighter load)
