# Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

Primary hamstring builder and essential hip hinge pattern.

## Why This Exercise

- Best exercise for hamstring development
- Builds glute-ham tie-in
- Strengthens posterior chain for injury prevention
- Teaches proper hip hinge mechanics
- Great stretch under load

## The Hip Hinge

The RDL is a hip hinge, not a squat:
- Movement comes from the hips, not the knees
- Think about pushing your butt back to touch a wall behind you
- Knees stay slightly bent but don't bend more during the movement
- Back stays flat - neutral spine throughout

## Range of Motion

Stop the descent when you feel a strong hamstring stretch. For most people, this is when the bar reaches:
- Mid-shin level
- Just below the knees

Don't go lower just to go lower. If your back rounds, you've gone too far.

## Grip Options

| Grip | When to Use |
|------|-------------|
| Double overhand | Light to moderate weight |
| Mixed grip | Heavier weight |
| Straps | When grip limits the movement |

Use straps freely - this is a hamstring exercise, not a grip exercise.

## Common Programming

- 3-4 sets of 10-12 reps
- Primary hamstring movement on leg days
- Can superset with leg curls
- Often programmed after squats

## Feeling the Hamstrings

If you don't feel your hamstrings:
1. Push hips back more
2. Keep knees more straight (but not locked)
3. Slow down the eccentric
4. Think about "showing your butt" to the wall behind you
5. Keep the bar closer to your legs

## Variations

- Dumbbell RDL (each hand, or single leg)
- Single Leg RDL (unilateral, balance challenge)
- Stiff Leg Deadlift (straighter knees, more hamstring)
- Good Morning (bar on back, similar pattern)
