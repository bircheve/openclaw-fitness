# Stairmaster

The mandatory cardio finisher for every workout.

## Why This Exercise

- Effective calorie burn without impact
- Builds leg endurance and conditioning
- Cardiovascular health benefits
- Mental discipline through sustained effort
- Doesn't interfere with leg recovery like running

## The Non-Negotiable Rule

Every workout ends with 30-45 minutes on the stairmaster. No exceptions.

- Heavy leg day? Still do it.
- Running late? At least 30 minutes.
- Feeling tired? Push through.
- "I did extra lifting"? Doesn't count.

## Intensity Guidelines

| Level | Heart Rate | Perceived Effort | Use Case |
|-------|------------|------------------|----------|
| 4-6 | 110-130 | Easy | Active recovery |
| 6-8 | 130-145 | Moderate | Standard session |
| 8-10 | 145-160 | Hard | Push days |
| 10+ | 160+ | Very hard | Interval training |

Standard recommendation: Levels 6-8, heart rate 130-150 BPM.

## Duration by Day

| Day Type | Duration | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Push day | 30-35 min | Standard |
| Pull day | 30-35 min | Standard |
| Leg day | 30 min | Legs are already fatigued |
| Big cardio day | 45-60 min | Extended session |

## Proper Form

The stairmaster only works if you do it right:

**Do:**
- Stand upright
- Drive through whole foot
- Take full steps
- Light touch on rails
- Maintain steady breathing

**Don't:**
- Lean on rails (this is cheating)
- Take tiny steps
- Hunch over
- Death grip the rails
- Look at your phone constantly

## Making It Bearable

30-45 minutes is a long time. Tips:
- Good playlist or podcast
- Watch something on your phone (mounted)
- Break it into mental chunks (3x10 min)
- Focus on breathing
- Think about your goals

## Heart Rate Zones

For fat burning and cardiovascular health:
- Target: 60-75% of max heart rate
- Max HR estimate: 220 - age
- Example (age 30): Target 114-143 BPM

## The Mental Game

The stairmaster is as much mental as physical.

When it gets hard (and it will):
- Focus on the next minute, not the total time
- Remember why you're doing this
- One step at a time
- Discomfort is temporary, results are permanent
