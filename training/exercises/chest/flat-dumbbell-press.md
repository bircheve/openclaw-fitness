# Flat Dumbbell Press

Mid-chest compound builder with greater range of motion than barbell.

## Why This Exercise

- Greater stretch at the bottom compared to barbell
- Each arm works independently (addresses imbalances)
- Easier on shoulders than barbell for some people
- Excellent for mind-muscle connection

## Dumbbell Selection

For 10-12 rep range:
- If you bench 185 lbs barbell, start around 70-75 lb dumbbells
- Adjust based on feel - DBs are harder due to stabilization

## Getting Into Position

1. Sit on bench, dumbbells resting on thighs
2. Lie back while kicking dumbbells up with thighs
3. Catch dumbbells at chest level
4. Set shoulder blades, then begin pressing

## Getting Out Safely

1. After final rep, bring dumbbells to chest
2. Sit up using momentum while lowering DBs to thighs
3. Stand up and return dumbbells to rack
4. Never drop heavy dumbbells from lockout position

## Variations

- Incline Dumbbell Press - upper chest emphasis
- Decline Dumbbell Press - lower chest emphasis
- Neutral Grip DB Press - easier on shoulders
