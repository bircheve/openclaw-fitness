# Barbell Curl

Classic bicep builder for arm mass.

## Why This Exercise

- Heavy loading potential for biceps
- Both arms work together (strength advantage)
- Easy to progressively overload
- Builds overall bicep mass

## Bar Options

| Bar | Wrist Comfort | Bicep Engagement |
|-----|---------------|------------------|
| Straight bar | Can be harsh | Full supination |
| EZ-curl bar | More comfortable | Slightly less supination |

If straight bar hurts your wrists, use the EZ-curl bar without guilt.

## Strict Form is Everything

Biceps are small muscles. You don't need much weight to grow them.

If you're swinging, leaning back, or using momentum:
- You're not training biceps
- You're risking lower back injury
- Drop the weight and do it right

The goal is to make the weight feel heavy to your biceps, not to move as much weight as possible.

## Range of Motion

- Bottom: Full extension, arms straight, feel the stretch
- Top: Full contraction, squeeze hard
- Keep constant tension - don't rest at bottom or top

## Common Programming

- 3-4 sets of 10-12 reps
- After back work on pull days
- Can superset with triceps
- Don't need to go crazy on volume - biceps get hit during all pulling

## The Ego Check

Your bicep curl weight doesn't matter. Nobody cares.

Use a weight you can control for 10-12 strict reps. If that's 50 lbs, great. If that's 70 lbs, great. Just don't cheat.

## Variations

- EZ-Bar Curl (easier on wrists)
- Dumbbell Curl (unilateral)
- Incline Dumbbell Curl (more stretch)
- Preacher Curl (eliminates cheating)
- Cable Curl (constant tension)
