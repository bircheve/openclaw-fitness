# Tricep Pushdown

Fundamental tricep isolation exercise for arm development.

## Why This Exercise

- Isolates triceps with minimal shoulder involvement
- Easy to establish mind-muscle connection
- Constant cable tension throughout
- Easy to adjust weight for drop sets
- Multiple attachment options for variety

## Attachment Options

| Attachment | Tricep Emphasis |
|------------|-----------------|
| Straight bar | All three heads |
| V-bar | Lateral head |
| Rope | Lateral head, more stretch |
| Single handle | Unilateral work |

## Elbow Position

The key to this exercise: **elbows stay pinned to your sides**

If your elbows drift:
- Forward: you're using shoulders
- Back: you're using momentum
- Out: you're going too heavy

Keep them locked at your sides throughout the entire set.

## Common Programming

- 3-4 sets of 12-15 reps
- After compound pressing on push days
- Great for drop sets and supersets
- Can train triceps 2-3x per week

## Drop Set Protocol

Tricep pushdowns are perfect for drop sets:
1. Working weight x 12-15 to failure
2. Drop 20%, immediately rep to failure
3. Drop 20% again, rep to failure
4. Massive pump guaranteed

## Mind-Muscle Connection

Think about:
- Straightening your arm, not pushing down
- Squeezing a pencil in your armpit (keeps elbow in place)
- Flexing your tricep hard at the bottom
- Feeling the stretch at the top

## Variations

- Rope Pushdown (spread at bottom for lateral head)
- Reverse Grip Pushdown (more long head)
- Single Arm Pushdown (unilateral)
- Overhead Cable Extension (stretch position)
