# Lateral Raise

Primary isolation movement for side delt development and shoulder width.

## Why This Exercise

- Best exercise for side delt isolation
- Builds shoulder width (the V-taper)
- Light weight, high volume movement
- Minimal joint stress when done correctly

## The "Pour Water" Cue

At the top of the movement, imagine pouring water out of a pitcher:
- Pinky higher than thumb
- Slight internal rotation of the shoulder
- This ensures side delt is doing the work, not front delt

## Common Mistake: Too Heavy

If you're swinging the weight, it's too heavy. Period.

Side delts are small muscles. Most people should be using 15-25 lb dumbbells for strict reps. If you can't do 12 controlled reps, drop the weight.

## Technique Variations

| Variation | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Standing | Standard |
| Seated | Less cheating possible |
| Leaning away | Greater stretch |
| Cable | Constant tension |
| One arm at a time | Better focus |

## High Volume Approach

Side delts respond well to high volume:
- 4-5 sets of 12-15 reps
- Drop sets work great
- Supersets with other shoulder movements
- Can train 2-3x per week

## Common Programming

- Often supersetted with pressing movements
- End of shoulder work for a pump
- Multiple sets, moderate-high reps (12-20)

## Variations

- Cable Lateral Raise (constant tension)
- Machine Lateral Raise (guided path)
- Leaning Lateral Raise (greater ROM)
- Lu Raises (front to lateral combination)
