# Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press

Primary overhead pressing movement for overall delt development.

## Why This Exercise

- Builds all three delt heads (front emphasis)
- Each arm works independently
- Easier on shoulders than barbell for most people
- Allows for greater range of motion

## Bench Angle

| Angle | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| 90 degrees (vertical) | Pure shoulder press |
| 80-85 degrees | Slight upper chest assistance, more comfortable |
| 75 degrees | More upper chest, less pure shoulder |

Recommendation: 80-85 degrees for comfort and safety.

## Getting Into Position

1. Sit on bench with dumbbells on thighs
2. Kick one dumbbell up at a time to shoulder level
3. OR have a partner hand you the dumbbells
4. Settle before beginning first rep

## Range of Motion

- Top: Arms extended, slight bend in elbows (don't lock out)
- Bottom: Upper arms at or slightly below parallel to floor
- Going too deep can stress the shoulder joint

## Common Programming

- 3-4 sets of 10-12 reps
- Primary push day movement after chest work
- Can superset with lateral raises

## Variations

- Arnold Press (rotation during press)
- Standing Dumbbell Press (more core)
- Barbell Overhead Press (heavier loading)
- Machine Shoulder Press (more stability)
