# Pull Day A - Back Width

Lat-focused pull day for building back width and the V-taper.

## Session Flow

1. **Warmup** (10-15 min)
2. **Lifting** (~75 min): Execute exercises in order
3. **Cardio Finisher** (35 min): Stairmaster at moderate intensity

## Exercise Order

| # | Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Lat Pulldown | 4 | 10-12 | 60s | Width focus |
| 2 | Barbell Row | 4 | 8-10 | 60s | Compound pulling |
| 3 | Single Arm Cable Row | 3 | 10-12 | 45s | Unilateral work |
| 4 | Straight Arm Pulldown | 3 | 12-15 | 45s | Lat isolation |
| 5 | Face Pull | 3 | 15-20 | 45s | Rear delt/upper back |
| 6 | Barbell Curl | 3 | 10-12 | 45s | Bicep mass |
| 7 | Hammer Curl | 3 | 10-12 | 45s | Brachialis/forearms |

## Execution Notes

- Pull with elbows, not hands - feel the lats
- All movements use 3-5 second eccentrics
- Last set of each exercise to failure
- Straps are allowed for back exercises

## Total Volume

- Back: 14 sets
- Biceps: 6 sets
- Rear Delts: 3 sets

## Mind-Muscle Connection for Lats

The lats are hard to feel. Tips:
- Think about pulling elbows down and back
- Imagine crushing something in your armpits
- Use lighter weight until you feel them
- Thumbless grip reduces bicep involvement

## Substitutions

If equipment unavailable:
- Lat Pulldown → Pull-ups or Assisted Pull-ups
- Barbell Row → T-Bar Row or Machine Row
- Single Arm Cable Row → Dumbbell Row

## Grip Considerations

Use straps when grip limits back work. This is a back day, not a grip day.

## Intra-Workout

Sip throughout lifting:
- 25g whey protein
- 10g BCAAs
- 5g glutamine

## Post-Workout

Within 10 minutes of cardio completion:
- 40g whey protein
- 32oz electrolyte drink
