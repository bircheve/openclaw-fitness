# Warmup Protocol

Standard warmup before every lifting session. Never skip this.

## Phase 1: General Warmup (5 min)

Elevate heart rate and increase blood flow to muscles.

- Treadmill walk at incline (3.5 mph, 6-10% incline)
- OR stationary bike at moderate resistance
- OR 5 min on stairmaster at easy pace

Goal: Light sweat, elevated heart rate, muscles feel warm.

## Phase 2: Dynamic Stretching (5 min)

Prepare joints and muscles for the movements ahead.

### Every Session (Hip Flexors + Mobility)
- Hip flexor lunge stretch: 30 sec each side
- 90/90 hip switch: 10 reps
- Pigeon stretch (or figure-4): 30 sec each side
- Leg swings front-to-back: 10 each leg

### Upper Body Days
- Arm circles: 20 forward, 20 backward
- Shoulder dislocates with band: 15 reps
- Band pull-aparts: 20 reps
- Torso twists: 20 total
- Cat-cow stretches: 10 reps

### Lower Body Days
- Leg swings front-to-back: 15 each leg
- Leg swings side-to-side: 15 each leg
- Hip circles: 10 each direction
- Walking lunges: 10 each leg
- Bodyweight squats: 15 reps
- Glute bridges: 15 reps

## Phase 3: Activation (2-3 min)

Wake up stabilizer muscles and prepare for heavy loading.

### Push Days
- Band face pulls: 2x15
- Band external rotations: 15 each arm
- Scapular push-ups: 10 reps

### Pull Days
- Band pull-aparts: 2x20
- Dead hangs: 2x15 seconds
- Scapular retractions (on bar): 10 reps

### Leg Days
- Monster walks with band: 10 each direction
- Clamshells: 15 each side
- Single leg glute bridges: 10 each side
- Goblet squat hold: 30 seconds

## Phase 4: Movement-Specific Warmup

Prepare for the first heavy exercise with progressive warmup sets.

### Protocol
- Set 1: Empty bar or very light, 15 reps
- Set 2: 40-50% working weight, 10-12 reps
- Set 3: 60-70% working weight, 6-8 reps
- Set 4: 80-85% working weight, 3-4 reps (optional for heavy days)

### Example: Incline Barbell Press (Working weight: 185 lbs)
- Set 1: Bar only (45 lbs) x 15
- Set 2: 95 lbs x 12
- Set 3: 135 lbs x 8
- Set 4: 165 lbs x 4 (optional)

### Rest Between Warmup Sets
- 30-45 seconds (just enough to add weight)

## When to Extend Warmup

Add extra time if:
- First workout of the day (cold muscles)
- Previous day was heavy (residual fatigue)
- Any joint feels stiff or tight
- Weather is cold (gym AC running high)
- Coming off a rest day or deload

## Skip at Your Own Risk

Skipping warmup leads to:
- Increased injury risk
- Reduced performance on working sets
- Poor mind-muscle connection
- Longer time to "get going" in the workout

The 10-15 minutes invested in warmup pays dividends in workout quality and injury prevention.
